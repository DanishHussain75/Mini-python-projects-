Current Catalog Description Today, it is increasingly important to make computer systems usable, useful and effective for
experts and novice users. The aim of HCI is to make systems that are easier to use, so that
people can achieve their goals faster, with less mistakes and greater satisfaction. It dictates
how a system reacts to what, and how Information is obtained and presented to the user.
Textbook (or Laboratory Manual for Laboratory Courses) Dix, J. Finlay, Abowd & Beale, Human-Computer Interaction, 2nd Edition, Prentice Hall

Course Goals Students will learn the fundamental concepts of human-computer interaction and User Centered Design, thinking through working in teams on an interaction design project, supported by lectures, readings, and discussions.
